Prabhat Ranjan Sponsorship SOP

Standard Operating Procedure broken down into 4 actionable phases.

Phase 1: Planning & Preparation (The Foundation)

1. Weekly Planning & Assignment
  • Target Lists: Each team (A/B/C/D) prepares a weekly list of target colleges.
  • Clear Ownership: Distribute colleges among associates; one associate per college at a time.
  • Ownership Change Rules:
    Mandatory Handover Process

    Mandatory: Document background, obtain Prabhat Ranjan’s permission, and update the Sponsorship Details sheet.

    Handover: Share call notes, emails, prior quotations, objections, and current status with the new associate.

2. Professional Presentation & Tone
  • Profile Hygiene: DPs (WhatsApp, Telegram, Insta) must be professional, mature, and neutral.
  • Avoid: Funky/cringe/meme visuals and casual selfies. (If unsure, remove the DP entirely).
  • Dress Code: Not formal, but must look decent and professional.
  • Communication: Use clear grammar, concise messages, and a standard email signature (Name, Designation, Channel).
3. Data Collection & Verification

Sources to Collect Data:

  • Official college websites (Admissions/Contact Us)
  • Friends, alumni, and professional networks
  • Google Maps (Admissions/Marketing office listings)

Mandatory Data Fields:

  • College: Official Name, Official Phone Number, Official Email ID.
  • Contact Person: Name, Phone, Email, Designation/Department (Prefer Admissions/Marketing/Management).
  • Verification: Prefer official domains (.edu/.ac.in). Cross-verify phone via website or receptionist.
4. Tracking Sheet & Color Coding (Mandatory)
  • Daily Check: Maintain and check the sheet every morning before outreach. Do not contact active colleges without permission.
  • Status Rule: Status must be a clear, actionable note (e.g., “Intro call done; proposal sent”).
  • Color Coding for Status:
    White: Contact not started yet.
    Yellow: Negotiation in progress.
    Green: Deal converted (include amount & date in Status).
    Red: Deal cancelled (include clear reason & date in Status).
    Blue: Restarted cancelled deal (mention context & current step).
  • Restart Rule: Speak to the previous associate and get permission from Prabhat Ranjan before restarting a cancelled deal.

Phase 2: Outreach & Negotiation (The Sale)

5. Outreach & Follow-up Discipline
  • Immediate Action: Send cold email blast, then start calls right after. Do not wait for responses.
  • Max Timeline: Follow-ups must conclude within 15 days maximum.
  • Thread Discipline: Keep every communication in the same email thread (Reply All).
  • Call Summary: Summarize call outcomes via email within 24 hours.
  • The 15-Day Follow-up Cycle:
    Progressive Value Sharing

    Day 1–3: Call + email reminder; share channel overview and outcomes.

    Day 4–7: Share value deck + indicative mixed pricing; position benefits.

    Day 8–12: Handle objections; propose tailored mixed model; confirm next steps.

    Day 13–15: Final push; schedule decision call; seek closure or formal next date.

    If not closed by Day 15, transfer the lead with background and get PR approval.

6. Core Negotiation Strategy
  1. Step 1 – Need Awareness: Message that colleges miss reach/trust/queries without strong social presence.
  2. Step 2 – Solution Positioning: Our audience, credibility, and content drive awareness, queries, and admissions (use prior collabs as proof).
  3. Step 3 – Payment Terms: Present models and insist on a mixed structure (Fixed + Commission).
7. Pricing Models & Thresholds (Goal: Mixed Fee)
  • Fixed Fee Minimum: ≥ ₹10,000 (Absolute floor, aim higher).
  • Mix Requirement: NEVER accept only fixed fee or only commission. Both must be present.
  • Discount Clause (Last Resort):
    50% Discount Condition

    Discount to ≥ ₹5,000 ONLY if the college also agrees to a commission-based model.

  • Per Admission (Commission):
    Commission Rules & Bands

    Requirement: Must provide a live admissions dashboard (Excel/manual tallies *not* accepted).

    Basis: Percentage of the total 4-year fee (Inst. + Tuition + Hostel + Mess).

    Bands: Minimum: 2% (threshold), Recommended: 5%.

    Evidence: Request detailed fee structure via email.

  • Per Lead Charges: Rate floor must be ≥ 1/20 of the per admission commission. Negotiate higher for premium programs.

Phase 3: Contracting & Finalization (The Agreement)

10. Contract Finalization & Sign-off
  • Decision Call: Arrange a call with Prabhat Ranjan and the college to finalize terms.
  • Contract Drafting: Fill the Sponsorship Contract Excel sheet completely and accurately.
  • Email Circulation: Send the filled contract to the college and Prabhat Ranjan (attach the sheet, keep the thread intact).
  • Kickoff Trigger: Wait for explicit email acknowledgements from both parties before starting production.
Contract Sections: Key Filling Rules
Section B/C: Contact Details
  • Use only Official Details (main line, official domain emails).
  • Contact must be empowered to approve budgets (Admissions/Marketing/Management).
  • Ensure consistency in spelling/designations.
Section D: Payment Agreement
  • Mark agreed contract types in GREEN background.
  • Enter amounts clearly (e.g., “INR 20,000 per Video”).
  • Mandatory Check: Validate Per Lead Charges ≥ (Per Admission Commission / 20).
  • Payment Cycles: Use consistent DD/MM/YYYY format.
11. Email Protocol for Contract Circulation
  • Recipients: TO: Prabhat Ranjan, College Contact Person. CC: College Official Email ID.
  • Subject: Sponsorship Contract between Prabhat Ranjan (YouTube Channel) and [College Name].
  • Action: Explicitly ask for email acknowledgment from both the Content Creator and the College Contact Person.

Phase 4: Execution & Follow-up (Delivery & Enforcement)

8 & 9. Payment Terms & Associate Incentives
  • Fixed Fee Best Term: Before video goes public.
  • Commission Payout: Seasonal (After admissions, ≈ September) or Monthly (1st of every month).
  • Associate Payout: 10% of total payment for deals converted.
  • Condition: Payout is only made after the college's payment is actually received.
12. Core Terms & Enforcement Rules
Payment Enforcement
  • Penalty: If payment is overdue, the video will be switched to Private until payment is cleared.
  • Escalation: If still unpaid 1 month after the due date, legal action may be considered.
  • Clock Start: Payment term countdown starts when video becomes Public (unless Before Public agreed).
Production & Revision Protocol
  • First Version: Delivered within 15 days (7 days recommended).
  • Review Window: College must respond within 7 days of receiving the Unlisted link.
  • Revision Cap: Maximum 2 revisions per video.
  • Revision Advance: INR 5,000 non-refundable fee required for any revision cycle.
  • No Response: If no reply within 7 days, contract may be cancelled without refund.
Lead & Commission Handling
  • Leads collected via Google Form, shared via Excel.
  • Admissions Dashboard: A live dashboard must be provided for commission verification (Excel/manual tallies are not accepted).
  • Commission statements shared monthly/seasonally.
Communication & Rights
  • Single Thread: Keep all communication in the same email thread.
  • Ownership: Content creator retains rights; college requires written permission for reuploads/edits.
  • Confidentiality: Pricing, commission rates, and lead data are confidential.
