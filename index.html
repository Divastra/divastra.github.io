FAQ

Questions We Hear Every Day

Honest answers to the questions every JEE aspirant asks.

Divastra is a free educational platform built by Prabhat Ranjan — a Mechanical Engineering graduate from NIT Jamshedpur. It's built specifically for JEE aspirants and B.Tech students who need real, practical guidance on college admissions, JoSAA counselling, branch selection, and career planning. Everything is free — no courses, no paywalls.
JEE Main is conducted by NTA and is the gateway to NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs via JoSAA counselling. JEE Advanced is conducted by IITs and is the only route to IIT admissions — you must first qualify JEE Main (top ~2.5 lakh candidates) to appear for JEE Advanced. Both tests have different syllabi and difficulty levels.
NIT cutoffs change every year based on the number of students, difficulty level, and seat availability — so no fixed percentile is reliable. Top NITs in CSE are the most competitive, while lower NITs and non-CSE branches are easier to get into. Category also matters a lot — OBC/SC/ST cutoffs are significantly lower. Use our JEE Rank Predictor for a real-time estimate, and always check the official JoSAA opening & closing ranks from the previous year for reference.
It depends on your goals. CSE leads to the highest-paying IT jobs and is the most in-demand. ECE is great if you want core electronics or software — it keeps both options open. Mechanical has strong core industry placements but lower IT sector salaries by default. College matters more than branch — a Mechanical degree from NIT Trichy beats a CSE degree from a weak private college.
JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority) conducts centralised counselling for IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs after JEE results. You fill your college-branch preferences in order, and seats are allotted based on your rank and category. There are 5–6 rounds. Good choice-filling strategy is crucial — booking a counselling session with Prabhat Ranjan can help you maximise your options.
Yes, absolutely. At 75–80 percentile, you may qualify for smaller NITs (in reserved categories), state government colleges via UPTAC/AKTU/KCET, and reputed private universities like LPU, Geeta University, Amity, etc. Many state counselling portals have good engineering colleges with decent placements. Don't just focus on JoSAA — explore state options actively.
IITs generally have better brand value, research culture, and top-tier placements. However, top NITs (Trichy, Warangal, Surathkal) are excellent and often match IIT placements in certain sectors. A newer IIT with a less preferred branch isn't always better than a top NIT with a strong branch. Always weigh institute reputation + branch + placement record together.
Core tools — the JEE Rank Predictor, Books Library, and Video Hub — are free to use. Some premium services like 1-on-1 personalised counselling sessions may have a cost, reflecting the time and depth of guidance involved. The mission is to keep the essentials accessible to every student. Check the counselling page for current details on what's free and what's paid.
